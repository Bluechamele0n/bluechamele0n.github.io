The Banishment of the Voidbringer

As remembered by Known

In the primordial days, when Fiondell still pulsed with the fresh breath of creation, I watched from the loom of memory as harmony reigned supreme among the Five. The gods—Fionil, Trill, Onigod, Flujack, and Tellemori—wove existence with confident hands. Yet even their grand design harbored an absence, a silent hollow at the fringes of light. From that abyss emerged Kryxis, the Voidbringer—a hunger given voice.

The Cry of the Void

I first heard Kryxis’s plea echoing through the Celestial Halls like wind through an empty cave. Its words were not thunderous, but a sibilant whisper that made the threadwork of fate tremble:

“You shape, you mold, you fill the void with your light. But you have left no place for me. Am I not part of the balance? Shall I be denied purpose?”

I felt the Tapestry shudder under the weight of that question. The Five gathered in the Hall of Threads, its vaulted ceiling a shimmering web of potential futures.

Fionil spoke first, her voice as steady as a clock’s tick:

“Kryxis seeks not harmony, but unmaking. Creation and void cannot both have dominion. We must shield the weave.”

Trill’s solemn tone wavered as he stroked a scale from a crystal drake’s wing:

“And yet… is it wrong to yearn for a purpose? Could we not grant Kryxis a place—a single stitch—so it might learn to uphold rather than erase?”

Onigod’s laughter rippled like magic unbound:

“To grant the Voidbringer a thread is to invite ruin. Its very essence corrodes. We cannot gamble the whole Tapestry on hope.”

The gods were at an impasse. So they turned to Destiny, Angel of Fate, the weaver whose courage equals his insight. I stood beside him, silent witness.

Destiny’s Gamble

Destiny stepped into the center of the Hall. The threads of fate hummed around him. I recall the hush that fell—time itself paused.

“Great ones,” Destiny said, voice resonant, “I offer compromise. Let me weave a single void-thread into the Tapestry. If Kryxis can sustain it—tend it without devouring all—then it may earn its place.”

Fionil’s gaze found his, inscrutable but tinged with hope. At last, she consented:

“Do as you will, my son.”

Destiny descended to the Loomhall. Before him lay the tapestry: strands of light, color, life, and possibility. With deliberate care, he plucked a sliver of nothingness from the Outer Expanse and wove it through the golden threads. A black thread appeared—fragile, cold, but stable.

Destiny turned to Kryxis’s shadowed form at the edge of existence:

“This is your chance, Voidbringer. Tend this thread. Show us you can coexist.”

Kryxis bowed, its hunger made hopeful promise:

“I will not fail.”

The Corruption Unleashed

For a time, the thread of void held, a mysterious rune woven into the pattern. But void is never content. Kryxis’s hunger grew. I watched in silent dread as the thread unfurled claws of emptiness, clutching neighboring strands.

Mortal realms felt it first: harvests rotted in the field, once-brilliant stars winked out, and nightmares took flesh—Wraithborn emerging at the edges of villages, feeding on sorrow.

I saw a farmer named Mara, tears streaming as her fields turned to ash. I saw children huddle in lantern-lit cellars, listening to their own hearts pound with terror. And I knew the Tapestry was unraveling.

The Divine Alliance

Fionil’s command rang across the void:

“Enough!”

Flujack answered with a tempest: skies rent with lightning so bright it seared the night. His thunderous decree:

“You shall not corrupt this world!”

Yet Kryxis’s shroud withstood the storm. Tellemori, earth’s heartbeat, struck next:

“By stone and river, I bind you!”

He raised mountains to trap the void, poured oceans to drown its spread. But the void seeped through cracks, corrupting all in its path.

In desperation, the Five called their angels. Destiny stood resolute:

“This is my burden. I led this gamble; I will end it.”

He led a host of angels into the breach:

  • Clock slowed the unraveling, turning moments backward to sew frayed ends.
  • Willia inspired mortals, granting them resolve to face encroaching despair.
  • Arcna, mistress of the arcane, wove binding runes around the void-thread.
  • Lucina descended in infernal flame, her judgment burning away the worst of the corruption.

Together—Light, Stone, Time, Will, Magic, and Judgment—they drove Kryxis back. Yet the void’s hunger was relentless.

The Final Banishment

In that dark hour, Destiny made his choice. He turned to Fionil, tears of starlight in his eyes:

“Goddess, we cannot destroy the Voidbringer—it is beyond unmaking. But I will bind it beyond all reach. I will sacrifice my place in the Tapestry to seal it in the Endless Expanse.”

Fionil’s sorrow resonated through every thread:

“If you do this, you will be sundered from fate itself. The Loomhall will no longer sing your name. You may be lost.”

Destiny’s resolve shone brighter:

“The Tapestry is not for me to own. I am its guardian, not its master.”

With the gods’ united power, they wove the final seal—a prison of unbroken light and unyielding time. Kryxis’s howls echoed into the void as it was drawn away, leaving only silence.

The void-thread snapped free, and the Tapestry shuddered as it healed, threads knitting beneath Destiny’s absent hand.

Aftershocks and Memory

I watched the world’s slow recovery. Fields regrew. Stars rekindled. Fearless mortals ventured forth, bearing scars of a darkness they barely understood. From this grim legacy rose the Shadowmarked—those whose veins carried a spark of void, a delicate power born of crisis.

Destiny returned—diminished, perhaps, but unbowed. He no longer wove every thread, but he wandered Fiondell, gently guiding fate with whispered nudges.

The gods honored his sacrifice. No trumpet blared; no chorus sang. Even gods speak softly of true miracles. Yet they vowed vigilance—lest the void’s hunger stir again.

And I, Known, have etched every moment into memory. The scars in the night sky—endless dark patches where stars once gleamed—are my silent reminder: balance demands both creation and watchful restraint.

In every age, when threads tremble, I remember Destiny’s choice:
Not to claim fate, but to protect it.

— Known, Angel of Knowledge and Eternal Witness
