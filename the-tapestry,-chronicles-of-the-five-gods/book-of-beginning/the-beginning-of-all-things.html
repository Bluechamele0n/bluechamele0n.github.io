The Beginning of All Things

In the beginning, there was the Vast Void, an expanse without form or purpose, an endless silence. From this emptiness, the Divine arose—five beings of infinite power and wisdom, destined to craft the fabric of existence. These were the Five Gods of Existence, eternal and boundless: Flujack, Trill, Tellemori, Fionil, and Onigod.

Each god bore a sacred mantle, embodying the essential forces that would sustain all creation:

  • Flujack, Keeper of Air, Sky, and Light, brought clarity and illumination to dispel the darkness.
  • Trill, Lord of Divinity, Dragons, and Life, was the fountain from which all living souls would spring, entrusted with the sacred cycle of birth and death.
  • Tellemori, Shaper of Water and Earth, laid the very foundation of existence, molding the seas, lands, and moons.
  • Fionil, Mistress of Time, Space, and Free Will, ordained the flow of moments and the structure of the cosmos, gifting mortals the power to choose their own paths.
  • Onigod, Weaver of Arcana and Keeper of Beasts, adorned creation with magic and wonder, her touch giving rise to creatures both majestic and fearsome.

Thus, the Five Gods stood as the architects of all things, bound by harmony yet each imbued with their own divine purpose.

The Order of Creation

And so, the gods began their sacred work.

Fionil, the First Mover, reached into the void and wove the threads of time and space. The heavens took shape, and the tapestry of creation stretched across the nothingness. She divided the endless expanse into realms, a stage upon which the other gods would cast their blessings.

Flujack, bearer of radiant light, ignited the first star, and the darkness fled before him. From his breath came the sun to mark the days, the moon to guide the nights, and the celestial spheres to adorn the heavens.

Tellemori, with hands steady and strong, sculpted the earth and seas. He raised mountains that touched the clouds and carved rivers that danced to the oceans. By his will, the moons and stars took their orbits, their movements marking time across the ages.

Trill, giver of life, exhaled the breath of vitality upon the world. He planted forests, set flowers to bloom, and commanded the beasts to rise from the earth and sea. With Fionil, he created the first dragons—creatures of unmatched wisdom and might, guardians of balance.

Lastly, Onigod, the arcane weaver, filled the world with magic. She crafted beasts of wonder and fear, monsters to challenge the strength of mortals, and mysteries to stir their hearts. She ensured that magic, both a gift and a trial, would forever shape the destiny of creation.

When their work was done, the Five Gods gazed upon the world they had made, and they declared it good.

The Angels of Creation

To maintain the balance of their creation, the Five Gods fashioned divine agents—angels, sacred guardians imbued with their essence.

  • Fionil, the Mistress of Time, created the first angels to uphold her design:
    • Clock, to watch over the eternal march of time.
    • Willia, to guard the freedom of choice.
    • Known, to grant wisdom to seekers of truth.
    • Filla, to guide the passions and emotions of all beings.
    • Destiny, the Hand of Fate, to nudge mortals toward their purpose while preserving their agency.
  • Trill, the Giver of Life, brought forth angels to tend the cycle of existence:
    • Livius, who ushered new souls into the world.
    • Death, who guided spirits to their eternal rest.
    • Prai, who inspired mortals to honor the divine.
    • Drana, the defender of dragons and the sacred bloodlines.
    • Lucina, who chose to govern the planes of darkness, becoming the first Demonlord.
  • Onigod, the Arcane Weaver, created guardians of magic and the monstrous:
    • Arcna, who bestowed the gift of magic upon mortals.
    • Poki, who shepherded the creatures of the wild.
    • Loki, who delighted in stirring mortal hearts with mischief.
    • Udean, who presided over the shadowy realms of undeath.
    • Cupin, who tempted mortals with sin to test their resolve.
  • Flujack, the Keeper of Light and Sky, appointed his angels to govern the heavens:
    • Day and Night, who kept the cycle of time.
    • Sandman, who brought the gift of sleep to weary souls.
    • Sky, who ruled the winds and storms.
    • Mr. Season, who wove the ever-changing tapestry of the year.
  • Tellemori, the Shaper of Nature, gave life to guardians of the wild:
    • The Lady of Fey, protector of the mystical realms of the fey.
    • Plantios, who nurtured the growth of flora.
    • Lady Love, who sowed harmony and affection among mortals.

Thus, the Angels stood as stewards of creation, their hands ever guiding, their presence unseen.

The Mortal Races and the Eternal Balance

In their final act of creation, the Five Gods shaped the mortal races—humankind, Elves, Dwarfs, Orc, and Halfling—each a reflection of their divine essence.

To house their souls and guide their destiny, Trill crafted the afterlife:

  • A realm of blessed peace, the Plane of the Gods, where the virtuous would dwell.
  • A realm of trial and penance, Damnation, for those who turned from the light.

In Damnation, Lucina, once an angel of light, became the first Demonlord, ensuring that even the wayward would find their path, however harsh.

And so, the Five Gods established the eternal design: a world of wonder and challenge, of light and shadow, of freedom and destiny. Their work was perfect, their wisdom eternal, and their love unyielding.
