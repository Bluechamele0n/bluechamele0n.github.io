The Temple of Demre

The Tapestry — Faith of Threads Eternal

The Tapestry is not merely a religion; it is the sacred pattern upon which the entire world rests, a divine weave of lives, fates, and cosmic intentions. Its followers believe every soul is a thread in a grand design spun by the angel Destiny, steward of fate and time’s unknowable pattern.

Demre Stade — City of Faith

Demre Stade is the beating heart of The Tapestry. Built upon an ancient, enchanted faultline where ley energies cross like woven strands, the city radiates divine resonance. Pilgrims pour into its sacred boulevards from every corner of the continent, seeking blessings, prophecies, or glimpses of the living embodiments of faith — the Pope, the Holy Knights, or even a whisper from the Angels.

Massive alabaster walls, inscribed with living scripture, encircle the city. Temples, courts, libraries, and pilgrimage houses form a maze of sacred institutions, all radiating around the Seat of the Twin Threads — a vast marble complex shared by the Pope and Queen.

The Temple Knights — Hands of the Divine

The Temple Knights are not just soldiers. They are judges, priests, paladins, bureaucrats, and prophets. Their armor is ceremonial and blessed, adorned with the crest of interwoven golden threads. They represent the law, the sword, and the will of both Queen of Demre and Pope of the Tapestry.

Total Number: ~11 million
Based in Demre Stade: ~500,000
Structure: Divine-Military Hybrid, split between Church and Crown

Joint Leadership

  • The Pope, the highest mortal voice of Destiny, serves as the divine guide.
  • The Queen of Demre, chosen by blood and ceremony, represents mortal governance and order.

Together, they are The Twin Threads — seen as the warp and weft of worldly and divine will.

The Hierarchy of the Temple Knights

  1. The Twin Threads – Pope & Queen of Demre
    The Pope speaks prophecy and issues holy decrees; the Queen commands the realms and defends the people. Together, they may ordain new Holy Knights.
  2. The Eleven Holy Knights – Emissaries of the Divine
    Each represents a principle thread in The Tapestry (e.g., Valor, Mercy, Judgment, Sacrifice, etc.). They are half-saints, given sacred names upon ascension. They are not merely leaders; they are symbols, legends, and champions all chosen by different angels.
  3. The Special Temple Guardians – Destiny’s Fangs
    A secretive force of 75 warriors chosen directly by Destiny, Archangel of Fate. They are half-seers, half-enforcers, acting on dreams, omens, and divine compulsion. Often Paladins of the Oath of Threads or Warlocks of the Fatebound Chain, they are Destiny’s personal instruments — feared and revered. Some call them the Threadcutters.
  4. Temple Commanders – Judges and Strategists
    Act as magistrates, generals, and interpreters of law. They often reside in regional temples, overseeing large sections of the world.
  5. Temple Guardians – Keepers of the Sacred Places
    These soldiers are stationed at holy sites, libraries, and relic vaults. They are trained not just in warfare but in lore and divine security.
  6. Temple Captains – Masters of Legions
    Each Captain leads a force of 250,000, a massive responsibility requiring logistical, spiritual, and military mastery. Their commands are akin to small kingdoms in their own right.
  7. Temple Leaders – Platoon Commanders
    Leaders of a unit of 100 knights, often selected from among proven warriors and acolytes. They maintain discipline and religious adherence.
  8. Temple Priests and Nuns – Spiritual Guides
    They maintain the rituals of The Tapestry, deliver sermons, and offer thread-readings (divinations of fate). Many are scholars of The Loomscript, the prophetic language of Destiny’s will.
  9. Temple Knights – Holy Enforcers
    The primary soldier-priests of the order. They act as both battlefield warriors and city patrollers. Their weapons are blessed, and their oaths are binding.
  10. Temple Watchers – Peacekeepers and Judges of the People
    These are the common faces of the Temple law. They wear simpler armor, yet their presence holds the force of divine mandate. They record all crimes in Threadbooks, which are said to be reviewed in visions by higher angels.

The Special Temple Guardians (The Threadcutters)

Chosen by Destiny himself, each of these 75 agents bears a mark — a glowing, shifting rune on the chest or forehead that pulses with strands of silver and violet light. They receive visions not from dreams but from weaving voids, glimpses into The Loom where threads tangle, rot, or unravel. Their mission is to cut those threads — people, events, or places — that would threaten the design.

Common traits:

  • Eyes clouded with faint silver threadmarks
  • Speak in prophetic riddles when under trance
  • Tattoos or scars shaped like broken spirals
  • Carry weapons made from Loomsteel, a rare divine metal said to sever fate itself

They are both Paladins and Warlocks, a hybrid not tolerated in any other order. Their patron is the Archangel Destiny, who offers no love, only purpose.

Additional Lore Hooks

  • The Loomvault: A hidden chamber beneath the Pope’s sanctum where the threads of saints and traitors are stored.
  • Threadfever: A madness that can afflict those who try to read fate without divine blessing. Symptoms include speaking in multiple voices and bleeding woven patterns.
  • Saints of the Spindle: Mortals whose lives impacted The Tapestry so greatly they became semi-immortal spiritual figures. Some Holy Knights are chosen from these.

1. The Weaving of Threads

Date: Midyear, on the longest day
Purpose: To symbolize that all lives are part of Destiny’s grand weave
Participants: The Pope, Queen, Holy Knights, and pilgrims

In this great rite, enormous looms are erected in the plaza outside the Seat of the Twin Threads. Silken threads representing the virtues of the Eleven Holy Knights (Justice, Mercy, Sacrifice, etc.) are woven into a new sacred tapestry by the Pope and Queen. Each thread color is said to have been blessed by a different angel. The final tapestry is hung for a year in the Cathedral of Looming Fate and then ritually burned to send it back into the Time Stream.

  • Invocation: The ceremony opens with the reading of the “First Stitch” — a poetic creation myth spoken by Temple Priests in a trance.
  • Blessing of Threads: The names of the angels are called aloud in an ancient language.
  • Public Participation: Children place symbolic threads into a side loom to represent their lives joining the weave.

2. The Flame of Damnation

Date: On the darkest night of the year
Purpose: To honor Lucina, Archangel of Damnation and reflect on death, judgment, and renewal
Participants: Priests of Trill, families of the deceased, and condemned criminals

A cold black flame is kindled from consecrated oil at midnight. This flame is said to be a piece of Lucina’s judgment. Names of the dead are read aloud, and people release ash-doves into the sky — folded paper birds burned in the black flame as offerings.

  • Forgiveness Ritual: Criminals and wrongdoers may choose to walk barefoot across the burning sigils of the Nine Chains of Hell and confess.
  • Lucina’s Silence: All bells, voices, and music are forbidden until dawn to honor Lucina’s still and solemn reign over the dead.

3. The Tranquil Tithe

Date: First new moon of spring
Purpose: To honor Fionil and her angels (especially Clock and Known), thanking them for the structure of time and knowledge
Participants: Scholars, judges, temple commanders

Each participant brings one book or scroll to be placed in the Library of Threads. In silence, they walk a spiral path and offer their work to Known, Angel of Knowledge, who is said to read every word. A glass orb filled with starlight is then placed upon a tower that records this knowledge in ethereal script.

  • Starlight Offering: Scribes read from ancient tomes aloud until sunrise. This is believed to slow the unraveling of fate.
  • Hour of Stillness: At midnight, no one moves for one full hour as tribute to Clock, Angel of Time.

4. The Verdant Chorus

Date: Mid-spring
Purpose: To honor Tellemori and Plantios, Angel of Flora, invoking fertility and healing
Participants: Temple Guardians, nuns, farmers, druids, and midwives

Fields outside Demre Stade are blessed with anointment oils and celestial seeds gifted yearly by Plantios. Songs are sung in shifting harmony, each representing the natural cycles. A single tree is planted in the Garden of Tellemori and blessed with water from all known rivers.

  • Procession of Growth: Children dressed as fey or animals dance through the streets led by a masked priest of Lady of Fey.
  • Night of Candles: Vows of love or reproduction are declared by couples. These are lit by candles representing Lady Love and watched over by Tellemori’s clergy.

5. The Oath of Fire and Scale

Date: On the summer solstice
Purpose: To honor Drana, Archangel of Dragons and War, and the celestial dragons
Participants: All Temple Knights, especially initiates

New knights are knighted by fire in a ritual pit of dragon-shaped braziers. They swear the Oath of the Tapestry before a statue of Drana and are marked with a glowing brand in the shape of a dragon's spiral tail.

  • The Wargame: Elite knights reenact ancient dragon battles using live magic and illusion to entertain and instruct.
  • Blessing of Blades: Each sword or weapon is dipped in burning oil said to be a gift from Ignarion, the Firefather.

6. The Severance

Date: Varies; done privately
Purpose: For the Special Temple Guardians (Threadcutters) who must sever a life or fate to protect the Tapestry
Participants: Only the Guardian, a senior priest of Destiny, and a silent witness

After following a vision from Destiny, a Threadcutter returns to Demre Stade and performs this solemn rite. In an underground chamber, they offer a prayer to Filla, the Angel of Feelings, to release emotional ties, then burn a thread marked with the name of the one whose fate they severed. Destiny does not answer; his silence is confirmation.

  • Thread of Silence: For seven days afterward, the Guardian may not speak.
  • Wine of Memory: They drink an elixir that causes temporary loss of identity — a symbolic death and rebirth.

7. The Ritual of Dual Sky

Date: The night when day and night are perfectly equal
Purpose: To honor Day and Night, twin angels of Flujack
Participants: Skywatchers, poets, clerics of the air

At dusk, all candles in the city are extinguished. Then, with a signal from the High Priest of Flujack, the city is relit by floating lanterns, each containing a single prayer written to Day or Night.

  • Nightglass Reading: Oracles gaze into obsidian mirrors to interpret signs from the changing sky.
  • Dance of Balance: Dancers dressed in white and black intertwine in circles to the rhythm of wind-chimes and flutes.

you who cradle both sinner and saint—

Let none drift beyond your gaze.
Let none escape your justice."
—Excerpt from The Fifth Flame, Book of Trill

"From thread to thread, from breath to breath,
Destiny sews what mortals guess.
The gods may speak, the fates may turn,
but all shall pass the weaving’s churn."
—Sung by the entire plaza at the climax of the ceremony

Knight's Oath of Scale and Fire (Oath of Fire and Scale)

Spoken by Temple Knight Initiates as they kneel:

"By claw and coil, by fire and fate,
I serve the roar, the war, the weight.
Let Drana test my mettle pure—
My oath, like dragons, shall endure."

The brand is then applied, and a senior knight proclaims:

"Rise now as flame-forged kin of dragonkind. Serve the Eleven, the gods, and the Tapestry eternal."

Fertility Blessing of Tellemori (Verdant Chorus)

"Bless this soil, and bless this soul.
May roots take hold, and hearts grow whole.
Under leaf and stone and stream,
let life awaken from its dream."
—Spoken by a druid priestess while watering the sacred planting tree

Vow of the Watcher (Tranquil Tithe)

Whispered by scholars before entering the Spiral Path:

"Known, who records, Clock who turns,
guide my hand so knowledge burns.
Let not a moment be unscanned,
nor a lie escape your watchful hand."

The Codex of the Tapestry

Also known as: The Loomscripture, The Eternal Weave, Threadbound Law

Foundational Divine Laws (The Ten Threads)

These are the guiding principles taught by temple priests and enforced by the Temple Knights:

  1. The Thread Must Not Be Cut – Life is sacred, and no soul may be taken without divine cause.
  2. The Weave is Shared – Aid your fellow beings, for their thread crosses yours by design.
  3. The Pattern Must Be Known – Seek truth, study history, and never suppress divine or arcane knowledge.
  4. Do Not Burn the Loom – Acts of reckless destruction, especially against nature or magic, are sacrilege.
  5. Threads Must Consent to Be Woven – Deals, pacts, and oaths must be given freely.
  6. Honor the Threads of Blood and Oath – Family, companions, and sworn brothers must be protected and upheld.
  7. Deny Not the Cycle – Birth, love, war, death—all must be embraced in their time.
  8. Do Not Disguise the Thread in Lies – False prophecy and impersonation of angels or gods is punished by excommunication.
  9. The Threads Must Be Judged in Kindness – Justice must be tempered with mercy, cruelty is a defilement.
  10. Remember You Are a Knot in a Greater Weave – No one is above the gods’ pattern.

Religious Vestments

1. High Clergy (The Eleven Threadmasters & the Pope):

  • Robes of Gleaming Thread: Silk-like gold and white with interwoven threads representing each god’s domain.
  • Thread-Crown: A circlet of braided platinum and opal strands, said to shimmer with fate.
  • Sash of the Eternal Pattern: Worn diagonally across the chest, embroidered with celestial runes and a stylized loom.

2. Temple Knights:

  • Threadbound Plate: Polished silver armor with gold-stitched undercloaks. The pauldron bears the sigil of their assigned angel.
  • Mantle of Oath: A crimson-and-silver half-cape bearing their order's mark and rank thread.
  • Ceremonial Brands: Dragon-scale tattoos or branded script across the collarbone for those sworn under Drana or Lucina.

3. Temple Priests and Watchers:

  • Vestments of the Loom: Modest robes in tones of pale blue, ash grey, and violet. Each has a single metallic thread trailing from shoulder to hem.
  • Mask of Contemplation: Ornate half-masks made of wood or ceramic worn during prayer or judgment.

Architecture of the Tapestry Faith

General Features:

  • Weaving Pillars: Towers or interior columns designed like spiraling braids or cords, often inscribed with timelines or bloodlines.
  • Threadglass Windows: Stained glass in curling spiral or loom-like patterns—each pane tells a part of history or a divine parable.
  • Skyloom Domes: Huge domes open to the sky with retractable cloth veils mimicking threads—used during eclipse rituals or holy nights.
  • Celestial Loom: A central loom or altar in each major temple with magically suspended threads representing fate, choices, or past/present.

Notable Structures:

  • The Cathedral of Endless Threads (in Demre Stade): The high holy site, housing the Eternal Loom—said to be woven by Destiny himself. Contains 23 sanctums, each for a known angel.
  • The Chapel of Silence: A temple without sound, dedicated to Fionil and Known. Worshippers must remain silent within, using embroidered prayer flags and hand signs.

The Code of the Tapestry Knights

“We are not swords. We are needles that thread justice through the cloth of the world.”

The Temple Knights—especially the elite Tapestry Knights—are both protectors and enforcers of divine law. Sworn to the Tapestry Faith, they follow a strict moral and spiritual code handed down from Destiny, Drana, and the Eleven Holy Knights.

The Sevenfold Code

  1. Oath Above All

“My vow is a thread in the divine pattern. I shall not cut it, burn it, or stray from its path.”

  1. Justice Without Cruelty

“I am not vengeance. I am resolution. I do not strike the unjudged, nor revel in pain.”

  1. Protect the Pattern

“The innocent, the faithful, and the holy are to be shielded as sacred stitches in the world’s cloth.”

  1. Duty Before Self

“I will lay down my glory, comfort, or life to preserve the Tapestry.”

  1. Truth is My Blade

“I shall not deceive with word or silence. Lies are thorns in the weave.”

  1. Mercy to the Worthy

“When I am able, I will offer redemption to the fallen. Severing a thread is the last resort.”

  1. Faith in All Things

“Even when lost, even in silence, I trust the loom continues.”

Divine Punishments & Atonements for Breaking the Threads

Breaking a sacred Thread (divine law) isn't just a crime—it’s a cosmic tangle, disrupting the will of the gods. Divine retribution is swift but offers paths to atonement. Punishments often manifest through visions, spiritual torment, or angelic intervention.

1. Cutting the Thread (Murder without just cause)

  • Punishment: Marked by Lucina with a fading shadow that follows them silently; those nearby feel ill in their presence.
  • Atonement: Offer soul service for one year as a Threadmender, a mute servant in a temple of Death or Lucina, caring for graves, ferrying souls, or slaying undead.

2. Lying in Judgment or Deceiving the Faithful

  • Punishment: Tongue turns to thread or mouth is sewn shut in a dream until truth is spoken.
  • Atonement: Public confession before a priest of Known or Fionil. A tapestry of their lie is woven in full detail and displayed in their local temple for 77 days.

3. Destroying Sacred Magic or Lore (Burning scrolls, erasing prophecy, harming wizards)

  • Punishment: Magic permanently misfires in their presence—can even cause random spell surges.
  • Atonement: Construct a Threadvault: a protected library or sanctuary for divine knowledge. Must offer up their own life story as its first scroll.

4. Breaking an Oath of Brotherhood or Knighthood

  • Punishment: Branded by a flaming chain across the chest by Drana’s spirit or an apparition of the Eleven.
  • Atonement: Fight in three hopeless causes for the weak—survive or die, but not flee. Upon the third, the brand will fade or transform into a badge of renewed honor.

5. Desecration of Sacred Places

  • Punishment: Dreams become haunted by angelic watchers, or the transgressor can’t walk upon temple grounds without burning pain.
  • Atonement: Must build or restore three shrines with their own hands. The final shrine must be to an angel they wronged.

6. Forcing Another into a Pact or Act (breaking Thread #5)

  • Punishment: Bound by fate—they are now doomed to obey one command from another, randomly triggered. Destiny rewrites their choices until they learn humility.
  • Atonement: Swear fealty as a Soulbound Servant to the person they wronged or to Destiny’s altar for one full season.

7. Abandoning the Weave (Renouncing the gods or trying to rewrite fate)

  • Punishment: Struck from the Loom—name removed from temple archives, fate lost, and no angel can hear their prayers.
  • Atonement (rare and difficult): Undertake the Pilgrimage of Threads, visiting all 23 angelic shrines, making a sacrifice at each, and letting Destiny choose if the thread may be rewoven.

8. Rending the Divine Coil (Killing a celestial dragon or dragon without cause)

  • Punishment: Marked by Drana and Araxiel with a serpent-like shadow that trails the sinner, causing all dragons to recoil or attack. Fate magic misfires around them, and their dreams are haunted by the dying breath of the slain dragon.
  • Atonement (arduous and sacred): Complete the Trial of the Wyrm’s Coil by raising a dragon’s egg to flight, offering a portion of their own soulfire at Drana’s altar, and earning forgiveness from a celestial dragon—only then may their thread be rewoven into the Loom of Fate.
