Fiondell

Fiondell is a world/ planet who is depicted in The tapestry. Fiondell was created by the five gods of creation and is monitored by them and their Angels.

Fiondell — The Living World of the Tapestry

Fiondell is the central world of The Tapestry, the grand creation woven by the Five Gods of Creation. Named after Fionil, the goddess of time, space, mind, and order, Fiondell is a vibrant, living world where divine power flows freely and mortal destinies unfold. This is a realm where swords clash, magic pulses, and the presence of gods and angels is deeply felt.

The Five Gods fashioned Fiondell as their masterpiece—a world rich with life, conflict, and endless stories. The gods rarely walk openly here, but their angels watch over the lands, guiding, protecting, and sometimes testing those who dwell below.

The Nature of Fiondell

  • Fiondell is a terrestrial planet with sprawling continents, vast oceans, and a varied climate—from frozen tundras to scorching deserts.
  • Magic is a natural force here, drawn from the weave of the cosmos and influenced by the god Onigod’s arcane design.
  • Mortal cultures vary widely across continents—some embrace magic openly, while others thrive on technology or strict military order.
  • The land itself bears scars of divine influence and ancient conflict: ruins of fallen empires, sacred ley lines, and places where the void’s dark whispers linger.
  • Celestial relics and artifacts of great power are hidden across the world, remnants of the gods’ handiwork and past battles.

The Continents of Fiondell

Fiondell is divided into many continents, each unique in culture, geography, and divine oversight. Some are fully mapped, others remain mysteries, and a few are shrouded in legend.

Varlen Brassleaf

Discovered during the Age of Water by the human prince Demre Brassleaf, Varlen Brassleaf proved the world held more land beyond the familiar seas.

Varlen Brassleaf is a continent where swords and magic intertwine. Before humans arrived, it was home to elves, dwarfs, orcs, and halflings. Over time, humans and others migrated here, weaving a diverse and often tense tapestry of races and cultures.

The continent’s cities reflect this diversity and history:

  • Demre Stade: Founded and declared an independent kingdom by Stade Brassleaf, son of Demre, at the close of the Age of Water.
  • Elven Forest: Ancient and fiercely protected by elves, the forest has stood since before the Age of Water and remains a place few outsiders enter.
  • Fishy Elifodep: A multicultural city created during the Age of Plants, intended as a haven for all races.
  • Med é Vill: A tribal orc stronghold known for its love of battle, predating the Age of Water.
  • Rapt Orlando: A halfling city famed for its peaceful nature and lack of violence.
  • Tinn Vill: The dwarven city centered on the continent’s only mine, where most dwarfs live beneath the earth.
  • Frostmá: A deadly island overrun by beasts and ruin.

Each city venerates its own interpretation of The Tapestry, reflecting the continent’s spiritual diversity.

Xeímor

A small, industrial continent shaped by steampunk technology and innovation. Only humans and elves live here, and they deny the existence of magic and other supernatural beings, though they unknowingly channel arcane energies to power their machines and inventions.

Dragoné, Island of dragons

A remote volcanic island inhabited solely by dragons and isolated dragonborn clans. The island is fiercely protected and governed by Drana, Archangel of Dragons, who also serves as the angel of war due to her fierce temperament.

Khorvaire — The Military Continent

Khorvaire is a land defined by constant conflict, strategy, and power struggles.

  • The primary angels overseeing this continent are Destiny and Drana.
  • Drana’s role as the angel of war deeply influences Khorvaire’s endless battles and volatile politics.
  • Contrary to earlier accounts, Prai has no connection to Khorvaire.

Fiondell’s Role in the Cosmos

Fiondell is more than a world—it is a living stage for the ongoing interplay of divine will, mortal courage, and cosmic mystery. The gods rarely intervene directly but influence the world through angels, relics, and the fates they weave.

  • The Five Gods’ presence is often felt but seldom seen.
  • Angels act as guides, protectors, or challengers for the peoples below.
  • Heroes of Fiondell shape the fate of continents, uncover divine secrets, and battle the creeping darkness left by the Voidbringer.

“In Fiondell, every leaf carries a story, every mountain holds a memory, and every soul is a thread in the great Tapestry of existence. The gods weave not only worlds but destinies.”
— Known, Angel of Knowledge
